Design Process

Overview

redily is a vintage clothing app that allows users to buy and sell sustainable clothing both online and in their neighbourhood. For the app target group (people between 25-34) sustainably purchased clothing is very important.

Key Functionalities

  • Create a profil
  • Upload items to sell
  • Leave reviews for other sellers
  • Favourite items: Show when sold
  • Set filter
  • Map showing searched products in the neighbourhood

Objective

The aim of the app is to make it easy for users to buy and sell vintage clothing, no matter where they are. In addition, the users can leave reviews for other sellers and see when a favourite item has been sold. With the help of a map, searched products in the neighbourhood are displayed and the user can see how long it takes to to pick up the item by foot, bike or car.

Research | Inspiration

At the beginning, I conducted a competitive analysis and made a general research. Here I learned from them and focused on their strengths. This way I was able to find solutions for my key functions.

Competitor Analysis

  • Tidy, clean design
  • Easy to navigate

Wireframing

With the inspirations in mind and taking design heuristics and design principles into account, I created the first low fidelity wireframes with pen and paper. First my Crazy 8 skills came into action.

Mid fidelity Wireframes

Syle Guide

In the next step, I developed the style guide to give my app a uniform character and recognition value. With the created stile guide I created a visual hierarchy.

Logo

The name comes from easy to find. Find and buy products quickly and easily. The logo consists of a hanger that symbolizes clothes. The leaf forms the bridge to sustainability. In the app, the buyer can directly look up how far away the product they are looking for is and how long it takes by bike or on foot.

Icons

The icon design is modern and simple. By using icons, users are navigated through the app more easily and filters are set faster. Together they form a family.

Colours

The colours are fresh and modern. In combination, they create a vintage character.

Typography

redily uses the typeface Niveau Grotes.

Buttons

Primary and secondary colours as well as a typographic hierarchy allow the user to navigate easily and quickly through the app.

Final UI

After creating the style guide, I implemented the images and icons into my screens. I defined a visual hierarchy with colours, typography and text and designed the primary and secondary buttons.

Retrospective

To find out which project tasks went well and which didn't go well, I did an agile retrospective. I analysed the workflows that could be improved and came up with solutions that will be considered in further iterations.

What went well

  • To create the icons was fun.
  • The exchange with my mentor were very helpful during the project. Through weekly calls, I was able to optimize the project regularly and complete it on time.
