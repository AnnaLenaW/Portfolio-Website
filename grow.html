overview

grow is a responsive web app that users can always use, no matter where they are and what device they have available. grow promotes a conscious approach to money. Moreover, grow helps to ensure that financial dreams do not just remain dreams but become reality.

Prototype

problem

There are very many finance apps on the market. However, most of them focus on general finances and stock market transactions. The user is also often expected to know about finances and independently invest money for individual wishes. There is no accessible app that focuses on savings and teaches the topic to financial newbies in a simple and motivating way.

solution

To ensure easy navigation through the app, users are familiarized directly with the most important functions with a onboarding after the login. This allows users to set savings goals quickly and without barriers. Created savings goals can be viewed on the main page and edited at any time. Since the app can be used as an advisor, tips are provided on all aspects of finance and saving. To reach the savings goal with an ease, motivational notifications are shown at regular intervals.

design process

user flows

I got the goals of my users and the scenarios from a project briefing. On this basis, I developed the User Flows.

  • As a user, I need to be able to tell the tool what my saving goal is and how long I have to reach it, so that I can save accordingly.
  • As a user, I want the money-saving tool I use to be motivational, so I stay on track with my goals.

low fidelity wireframes

My first sketches for the user flow with pen and paper.

user testing

I tested my mid fidelity wireframes on five test users. The aim was to identify where the participants were doing well and where they had problems.

Information for my test users

grow is a tool that helps you save money easily and quickly. With the help of a savings plan, you can save money for a special purpose in a certain period of time.

Scenario

You need a new mountain bike for next summer. It costs 1,500 € and you have 12 months to save for it. Please create a savings plan to reach this goal.

user feedback

I identify the most important pain points,of my user feedback. I prioritize them based on their level of necessity (critical or nice-to-have).

feedback analysis

  • First I have to focus only on my user flow “crating a saving goal”
  • Fix errors on the login page
  • It must be possible to finish the setup faster - users are unsatisfied if they have to click through categories
  • Setting the time period for the savings goal must be flexible so that the user can better estimate the amount to be saved.
  • The savings goal must be editable
  • Use consistent wording

high fidelity wireframes

After I analysed the user feedback and prorised it in the error rating, I moved on to the high fidelity wireframes. These were further kept in greyscale to ensure intuitive usability.

style guide

After creating the high fidelity wireframes in greyscale I proceeded with the design of the final UI. First I designed the grow brand guidelines. This sets out all the guidelines of the brand, ensuring consistency across all channels.

grow Brand Guidelines

colours

The color palette of grow consists of different blue tones which communicate security and trust. They are combined with dynamic accent colours. These colours help to develop joy and fun in finance.

typography

The type font of grow is Poppins. Poppins is a sans serif typeface. It looks solid and thus conveys confidence and security. Especially in finer font families, it creates a feeling of lightness.

logotype

Our logo grow is a protected word mark and must always be written in lower case. The “w” additionally visualizes a rising, positive course. On the one hand grow stands for the growing savings that can be reached with the help of the app, and on the other hand for the growing knowledge of the user.

To maintain the integrity of the brand it is important to present the wordmark in the best possible way in terms of contrast and legibility. The examples demonstrate acceptable usages and colour combinations.

final UI design

After creating the brand guidelines, I applied the guidelines to my high fidelity wireframes and designed the final UI in Figma.

retrospective

What went well

  • The development of the brand style went well.
  • The feedback from the users has been good.
  • Watching several tutorials has been worthwhile. My skills in Figma are improving. I gained a good knowledge of components.
  • This time I had a better time management. I was able to finish the project easier and faster.

What can be improved

  • The colours from the style guide were not applied consistently at the beginning. It took a few iterations until the colour consistency was right, and the user guidance became clear.
  • From the first user feedback, it became clear that the user guidance was still very confusing. In the next iterations, the complexity was broken down and simplified. In the future, user surveys should always be conducted before user flows and wireframes are developed to save time and costs.

What went wrong

  • Nothing went unusually wrong with this project. Through quick feedback from my users and frequent calls with my mentor, I was able to avoid major mistakes.
